A bit about me...

I enjoy making things organized, efficient, and streamlined with a little something extra to bring about a smile.

Like many Content Designers, I have a background in something unexpected.

After receiving a B.S. of Community Health, I spent several years in the public health & non-profit sectors. I became interested in how new tech was able to share health data in insightful ways. So I switched careers to something completely unfamiliar: software engineering.

I was introduced to UX while working as a software engineer building out features of a vehicle head unit interface. It was inspiring to see intuitive experiences artfully constructed for such a constraint-laden space.

While English is my first language, it was one of my parents' second. So growing up, I’ve always been interested in how concepts are communicated. There’s a power not just in words, but also in the context in which those words exist. How we communicate can be empowering or excluding.

So, here I am to write better experiences for everyone.
