Pillsy - "Add a Helper" Flow

Content revision

The Problem

Pillsy is an app that helps people track their medications and supplements. In addition to setting a medication schedule and reminders, a user can also add a “Helper” who will be notified via SMS if they miss a scheduled dose. The current flow for adding a helper assumed that both the user and the helper were familiar with the app and knew exactly what to expect for the role of a Helper.

By having these assumptions written into the app experience, the problem is that it assumed a “perfect use case” and missed offering options, confirmations, and information that would instill confidence in the product for both the app user and their helper.

What I Did

  • UX copy audit
  • Content redesign
  • Microcopy
  • User testing

The Solution

An audit of the UX copy revealed:

  • Repetition of a general overview of the feature (for the user)
  • No specific details of what a helper could expect (for the user)
  • Lack of confirmation and error messaging (for the user)
  • No introduction/context of Pillsy (for the helper)
  • Not enough options/support around SMS (for the helper)

Focusing on these areas, I edited the copy and made adjustments to the flow, formatting, and copy design to address how the information was being presented.

To test the changes, I used a highlighter confidence test in an AB test format to compare the original and revised versions. While I gained even more insights for further ways to improve the UX copy, initial testing with 6 individuals revealed that confidence improved overall for both app users and their helpers.

The Big 'So What?'

Confidence in the app increased by 8% for Pillsy users and 11% for their helpers.

To make assumptions that a user will follow the flow as a perfect use case every time means being underprepared for handling stress cases. When thought about early, there is time to preemptively create an empathic experience through less-than-ideal circumstances.
