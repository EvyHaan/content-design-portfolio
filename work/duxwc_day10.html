Daily UX Writing Challenge: Day 10

Data collection

(concept piece)

The Problem

The Scenario

The user is trying to view a website to help them buy a car. But, the content can’t load without the user’s location. They need to enter their ZIP code and first name.

The Challenge

Ask them where they live and who they are without sounding like you're unnecessarily mining their data.

Headline: 25 characters max
Body: 45 characters max
Button: 15 characters

What I Did

  • UX copy design

The Solution

While it is clear to me why obtaining the user’s location is valuable here, it’s less clear why I also need their first name. Even operating on the assumption that the user ultimately will create a profile to manage their searches, this form implies a different immediate intent. I would take this question back to the PM.

Until then, the language is simple and honest about what the information will be used for. I use a tone of familiarity and camaraderie by using a first persona plural point of view, as an invitation. The user’s feelings may range from excitement to dread at the process of car-shopping. The site’s voice is reassuring that we will support them through the process.

The Big 'So What?'

Piecemeal data-gathering may make a user wary of the service. If we are asking a user for personal information, explaining why we need it is not enough. The order and timing of our ask must also be presented in a way that clearly aligns with how we’ve said we’d use it.
