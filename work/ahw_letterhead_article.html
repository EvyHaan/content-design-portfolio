"Should Board Members Be Listed On Organization Letterhead?”

(Article)

The Problem

This question came up while working on the visual brand guide for the Alliance for Historic Wyoming (AHW). The current letterhead featured a list of board members and their locations, but the decision to keep it was being challenged by the staff who maintained it.

There were several challenges:

  • Staff time as a finite resource
  • Frequently changing board members
  • Changes often undermining the strategy behind the decision
  • A design that wasn’t conducive to maintenance

What I Did

  • Research
  • Write an Article

The Solution

My goal was to bring both sides together for a meaningful, objective conversation. I framed a response in terms of the return on investment (ROI) for implementing this branding element within a marketing strategy. By examining each aspect of investment in the design and upkeep of this element against the purpose of using the element in the first place, it’s easier to gauge a truer personal value of the ROI.

The Big 'So What?'

Returning to the strategic plan can be grounding for different teams that are working toward a common goal. Stepping the conversation back to an overview level can help realign perspectives and provide the objectivity needed for making challenging decisions.
