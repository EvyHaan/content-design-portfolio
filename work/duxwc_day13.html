Daily UX Writing Challenge: Day 13

Notification

(concept piece)

The Problem

The Scenario

A short-haul truck driver has a phone app that monitors his route, schedule, fuel & deliveries. He has 6 more deliveries before stopping for fuel and lunch. Due to unexpected traffic, heâ€™s behind schedule.

He can choose to stay on his planned route for a few more stops, but risk running low on fuel and missing lunch, or he can get fuel and lunch now and finish the deliveries later.

The Challenge

Write a push notification alerting him of this dilemma and options.

Title: 30 characters max
Body: 45 characters max
Button(s): 25 characters max

What I Did

  • UX copy design

The Solution

Considering the current activities of the user (driving), I want the message to be scannable and succinct. The important messages are:

  • Heavy traffic
  • Remaining deliveries
  • Fuel level (optional - if the device & app is set to receive telemetric data)

I would want to ask the PM about the trigger for this push notification event. Is it detected heavy traffic? Is it the remaining fuel? Is it a routine, timed status update? That would help determine the priority, order, and visual hierarchy of these messages.

I convey the options the driver has in a matter-of-fact tone. The provided scenario presents the driver is already under pressure from being behind. However, this message may appear at other times, so they should be neutral to make sense in any situation.

The Big 'So What?'

Content design is a collaborative process alongside product design, visual design, and customer research. Content design is more than the words used. Timing, trigger events, visual display of the words, and perceived importance all play a role in conveying the right message at the right time.
