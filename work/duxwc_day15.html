Daily UX Writing Challenge: Day 15

Onboarding flow

(concept piece)

The Problem

The Scenario

A user uses a mobile banking app. It has an automatic bill-pay feature. The user has not yet set this up although they think it would be helpful in managing their finances.

The Challenge

Write a multi-screen onboarding experience for a banking app that automatically pays a user's bills every month—as long as they set it up correctly.

Headline: 45 characters max
Body: 100 characters max
Button(s): 25 characters max

What I Did

  • UX copy design

The Solution

My goal was to make the onboarding process as close as possible to the ongoing-use flow. Extra instruction and microcopy would help answer questions a user might have as they are becoming familiar with the tool, but the onboarding process should also be practical and meaningful.

I limited the introduction of the feature’s benefits to the initial landing screen. I use the rest of the onboarding flow to walk the user through setting up their first automatic payment. Each screen focuses on one idea, asking the user to make decisions or complete tasks related to that one concept.

Finally, I felt it was important for the user to have the option to opt out of the guided setup. Each screen has the option to skip that step. Some users may appreciate guidance. Others may be interested to view the dashboard. They may want to investigate the functionality and options to determine if it is the right tool for them, before they invest too much time in setting it up.

The Big 'So What?'

Just because someone is new to our app, does not mean that they are new to digital tools. When we write flows for a broad audience, we must keep in mind the equally broad range of technical capacity. Onboarding flows may tread a fine line of assisting users who need help to move forward while trying to not slow down the momentum of others.
