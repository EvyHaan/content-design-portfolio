Daily UX Writing Challenge: Day 4

Promotion Screen

(concept piece)

The Problem

The Scenario

A user is in their favorite supermarket. They open the supermarket’s app on their phone to see what’s on sale and are greeted by a promotion.

The Challenge

Write a promotional home screen for a subscription service that delivers groceries to the user once-a-month for a flat fee.

Headline: 45 characters max
Body: 175 characters max
Button(s): 25 characters max

What I Did

  • UX copy design

The Solution

I created a promotional interstitial.

There are two main messages I hoped to convey:

  1. Whatever your reason for needing this service, we’re here for you.
  2. This service is easy to use.

Up front, I do not include speculative rhetoric about what benefit they might find in the service. We could guess common themes. But some users may have uniquely personal needs and can imagine for themselves the most compelling benefits for their lifestyle.

I assure them how easy the service is to use by outlining the in-app steps. This gives the user a sense of the time and effort commitment as well as preempts questions like “when do I get my groceries?” and “can I still use coupons?”.

The Big 'So What?'

People know what they want to get out of a service. They identify benefits on a personal level. But they may struggle to find the right service. We can lower the barrier of the unknown by telling them what to expect. Allowing a level of commitment-free transparency can immediately establish customer rapport.
