Daily UX Writing Challenge: Day 12

Error message - form field validation

(concept piece)

The Problem

The Scenario

A user is creating an account. When they come to the step where they are asked to enter their name, they get an error message. A fraud detection software thinks their name is fake—but it’s wrong 5% of the time.

The Challenge

Challenge: Write an error message that prompts them to fix the error without shaming them for having a fake-sounding name.

45 characters max

What I Did

  • UX copy design

The Solution

Microcopy under the relevant form field keeps the context for the error close. We can make the copy work harder by linking it to additional information to help the user.

There are so many edge cases to consider here:

  • Minimum character count
  • Special characters like hyphens or spaces
  • System-reserved keywords
  • Derogatory terms

I remain objective in telling them what the general problem is, because I don’t have the space here to detail all the possible reasons. I also try to convey that our policy is a living document, and hint that we are constantly iterating on it for the better. I provide a link where they can get clarity and find support to overcome the issue.

The Big 'So What?'

User research is invaluable to the health of a service or product. We must look forward to feedback from our customers and be willing to recognize when it’s time for change.
