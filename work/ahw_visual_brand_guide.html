Visual Brand Guide

(Volunteer project)

The Problem

The executive director at Alliance for Historic Wyoming (AHW) wanted to combine the organization’s expanding set of brand artifacts into a single, easy-access location with guidelines for use.

The unique challenges were:

  • Design decisions that weren’t resonating
  • Style specifications that were defined in multiple documents
  • Limitations imposed by different software, tools, and platforms

What I Did

  • Content design
  • Visual design
  • Asset creation

The Solution

The executive director and I discussed characteristics of the organization, the decision making process and past design decisions, limitations or points of friction related to design elements, and her vision for the end product.

I designed a brand reference manual as a Google Slides deck. This was a familiar, low-maintenance format that would be easy for AHW to access and share. I organized additional assets and instructions in a Google Drive.

Personalizing the manual was a primary objective. Board members contributed keywords that they felt represented the work of the organization. I weaved these into topic introductions and descriptions, along with visually descriptive language reminiscent of Wyoming icons, landscapes, and history.

The Big 'So What?'

A visual brand guide is more than a document. It’s the story of how an organization shows up in its community. It’s a reminder for why past design decisions are still relevant. It’s an optimist, looking forward to leaving an impact on future audiences. And it’s a collaborative partner to the staff who rely on it daily.
