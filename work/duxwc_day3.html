Daily UX Writing Challenge: Day 3

Error message - form field

(concept piece)

The Problem

The Scenario

The user entered the wrong email address to sign in to their account.

The Challenge

Tell the user to enter the right email.

40 characters max

What I Did

  • UX copy design

The Solution

Microcopy below the form field is the best practice in this situation.

There’s really two scenarios to cover here: is it the wrong email, or is the email wrong? All we know is that it doesn’t match any email in our database. This isn’t an interaction where a user wants to be engaged for long. So, with a neutral tone, I succinctly convey the problem and what to do next.

However, we must also keep in mind our user’s security. We do not want to reveal too much about what exactly failed, as it may make a user more vulnerable to hacking. In this situation, giving precedence to security over designing a completely seamless UX, has a high high benefit to cost ratio.

The Big 'So What?'

A frictionless UX may not always be a good UX. Security flows can require extra steps and vague language. But ultimately, a security breach is going to be a bad user experience. It is in our best interest, and in our user’s best interest to protect the information they trust us with. Even at the cost of a little extra effort.
