Daily UX Writing Challenge: Day 8

In-app promotion

(concept piece)

The Problem

The Scenario

The user is a casual music fan and (on occasion) goes to live concerts. They have a music player app on their phone.

The Challenge

Tell the user that one of their favorite bands is playing live in their town. How would you compel them to want to go?

Headline: 30 characters max
Body: 45 characters max
Button: 25 characters

What I Did

  • UX copy design

The Solution

In considering the design system of an app, I thought an in-app advertisement ‘card’ was appropriate over an interstitial page. If a user closes an interstitial, they may not know how to return to that information later.

I wanted to consider the entire app ecosystem, to align the advertisement with the existing design system for a cohesive feel.

The design contains easily scannable information. Its layout is effective to communicate the essential details in a format that is easy to duplicate. The written content might be scant, but it lets the cover photo on the card shine to drum up the excitement.

While similarly designed feature cards are tappable elements, this card is not. The CTA occupies a smaller area underneath, to reduce the chance that a user may accidentally tap it while scrolling.

The Big 'So What?'

Design systems are important for building consistency across every aspect of an app. They lend to reduced cognitive load on the user by offering a mental shortcut through tying concepts and interactions to recognizable patterns. They also serve as a guide for expanding features that feel true to the personality and experience.
