Daily UX Writing Challenge: Day 1

Cancellation notification

(concept piece)

The Problem

The Scenario

A traveler is in an airport waiting for the last leg of a flight home when their flight gets abruptly canceled due to bad weather.

The Challenge

Write a message from the airline app notifying them of the cancellation and what they need to do next.

Headline: 45 characters
Body: 175 characters max
Button(s): 25 characters max

What I Did

  • UX copy design

The Solution

A push notification seemed appropriate for this scenario, for urgency and brevity.

The customer is likely feeling annoyed, frustrated, &/or frantic. We (the airline) want to meet them with empathy and be direct on next steps. In a short space, I try to communicate:

  • What happened
  • Why
  • That their feelings are valid
  • Next steps (options for paths forward)
  • Something positive (this may not always be the case)

There may not always be a reason ‘why’ that the airline can share with the customer. For inclement weather, it does feel appropriate. It provides the customer an important context that may factor into how to proceed with making new arrangements.

The last two ideas are dependent on what the airline process may be. As a UX Writer, I’d need to work with our customer services team to find out exactly what the airline is able to offer. Of these, I felt strongly about offering the customer different paths forward. They may be digitally savvy and able to move forward with minimal assistance. But they may not be. Or they are until they are in a high-pressure situation. We’d like to respect that preference and meet them where they are most comfortable, especially when they are in a lurch.

The Big 'So What?'

Even in negative situations, there’s room to build trust. Approaching these critical scenarios with empathy and transparency goes a long way. For the customer, what matters is that they don’t feel abandoned. For the company’s, it’s an investment toward returning customers.
