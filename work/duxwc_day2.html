Daily UX Writing Challenge: Day 2

Promotion screen

(concept piece)

The Problem

The Scenario

A user is a working parent, and a big sports fan, in the midst of their favorite sports season who can no longer attend games.

The Challenge

Write a promotional screen for an app that lets a user choose teams, sends game reminders, real-time score updates and highlight videos.

Headline: 40 characters max
Body: 175 characters max
Button(s): 25 characters max

What I Did

  • UX copy design

The Solution

I imagined this as an onboarding screen. The user is brimming with excitement to still be an active avid fan. They have just installed and opened the app.

There are three messages I wanted to communicate:

  1. Commending them for their dedication to the game, the team, and the community.
  2. Reaffirming that they’ve chosen the right app: We have the features that’ll get them as close to the game as possible, when they can’t physically be there.
  3. Assuring them that the app is a low-effort commitment. All they do is set it and we’ll never let them forget it (in a good way).

With that last idea in mind, the CTA cuts right to the action. They’ve found a moment to search for and install an app, so it's likely also a good time to get them onboarded where they might not have the luxury of a free moment again for a while.

The Big 'So What?'

There’s a reason why users look for apps. Listing features of an app can be a great draw. But even greater value is the benefits to the user in terms of the impact it has on their current life context.
