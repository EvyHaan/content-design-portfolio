Daily UX Writing Challenge

Daily UX Writing Challenge is 15 days of new UX Writing prompts to practice writing quick, helpful user-oriented copy. Find out more on the Daily UX Writing website.

Day 1


Scenario: A traveler is in an airport waiting for the last leg of a flight home when their flight gets abruptly canceled due to bad weather.


Challenge: Write a message from the airline app notifying them of the cancellation and what they need to do next.


Headline: 45 characters

Body: 175 characters max

Button(s): 25 characters max


Solution:



Explanation:


In the headline, I wanted to be direct with the customer about 1) what had happened, and 2) why. Understanding why may not necessarily ease frustration. But given more context, a user can have a better sense of what to consider when figuring out what to do next.


There are three messages in the body. First, we empathize with the customer. They may be feeling annoyed, frustrated, or frantic. I want to acknowledge and validate those feelings.


Next, I tell the user what to do. I let them know they have two options. They can use the app to rebook their flight. Or, if they'd be more comfortable speaking with a person to navigate a stressful situation, we'd like to respect that preference.


Finally, I offer them something to look forward to.


The button gives them the opportunity to jump straight into rebooking, starting with viewing other flight options.

Day 2


Scenario: A user is a working parent, and a big sports fan, in the midst of their favorite sports season who can no longer attend games.


Challenge: Write a promotional screen for an app that lets a user choose teams, sends game reminders, real-time score updates and highlight videos.


Headline: 40 characters max

Body: 175 characters max

Button(s): 25 characters max


Solution:



Explanation:


With the headline, I wanted to celebrate the user for balancing all they have going on in life while still being a devoted fan.


Because, this is a peron without a lot of time, I am direct with the beneifts. I also assure them it's a low-effort relationship with the app. Set it and we'll never let you forget it (in a good way).


The CTA cuts right into the action to get them set up.
