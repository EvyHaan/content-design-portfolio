Content Design Philosophy

Communicate from the head, the heart, time, & space.

Concepts are tinged with emotions and imagination. They develop over time from countless experiences. One concept could be understood a thousand ways depending on who is examining it and the nuances they bring.

Communication starts with listening. It is the most effective when the foundation is understanding how someone else understands.

Journey to UX

Like many Content Designers, I have a background in something unexpected.

After receiving a B.S. of Community Health, I spent several years in the public health & non-profit sectors. I became interested in how new tech was able to share health data in insightful ways. So I switched careers to something completely unfamiliar: software engineering.

I was introduced to UX while working as a software engineer building out features of a vehicle head unit interface. It was inspiring to see intuitive experiences artfully constructed for such a constraint-laden space.

Growing up, I’ve always been interested in how concepts are communicated. While English is my native language, it was one of my parents' second. I am amazed at the flexibility and tickled by the nuances of different forms of communicating. There’s a power not just in words, but also in the context in which those words exist. How we communicate can be empowering or excluding.

When I discovered the UX writing / content design / content strategy space, I was excited to make another career jump. I have loved diving into the psychology of communicating in the digital space, human-computer interaction, and dabbling with concepts of ontology.
